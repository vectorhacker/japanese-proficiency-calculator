⚠ Important caveats
  1. Proficiency is multi-dimensional. Vocabulary, grammar, listening speed, kanji recognition, and productive ability rarely sit at the same level for any one learner.
  2. "1,000 words" on JGR is not the same as "1,000 words" on JLPT vocab lists — the sets overlap heavily but aren't identical. Conjugated forms and counting methods differ.
  3. Duolingo's Score is course-completion based; it measures progress through the app's curriculum, not raw proficiency. The Japanese course doesn't currently expose the Score.
  4. CEFR was designed for European languages. Its application to Japanese is a recent adaptation and remains an active research area — N5 ≈ A1 is a useful approximation, not an equivalence.
  5. The CIJ difficulty score is a statistical model of video transcripts. Topic familiarity, visual scaffolding, and personal interest can shift effective difficulty up or down by 10–15 points.
